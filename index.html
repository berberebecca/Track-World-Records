What Olympic running event do you need to watch to see a new world record?

Statistically speaking.

By Rebecca Habtemariam

Winning Olympic Gold may be one of the biggest archievements possible for a profesisonal athlete. And sometimes they not only triumph over their peers that are competing with them in that moment, but also over the ones that came before them - aka, they break a record. An Olympic one, sometimes even the world record.

But how often does one break a world record? In this article we're looking specifically at the runners and their world record progressions. For the records to be included in the following statistics, they need fulfill the following three points:

  • Record needs to be recognized by the World Athletics, which is the global athletics federation, or the Association of Road Racing Statisticians
  • Needs to be a a Olympic discipline (as of Olympics 2024)
  • Only the automatcally timed records in categories, where they became obligatory at some point

The data has been scraped from Wikipedia (on the 29.07.24), since the tables have been practical to scrape. The information has been cross-checked with data from World Athletics. First, lets look how many times a record within a discipline has been broken - first the male and afterwards the female runners.

In the last 20 years there have been multiple world records from male long- as well as short distance runners.

The most current world records amonst women are all long-distance runners.

It has been a long time since a female short track runner was able to break a new world record. The records over 100 and 200 metres sprint are both still from Florence Griffith-Joyner aka Flo-Jo from the year 1988. According to this statistics it would be more likely to see a new female world record within the long-distance runners. Amongst the men, a clear pattern doesn't seem to be very visible yet.

The biggest opposing argument when trying to argue with a mean value is a spike - In this case an athlete who has broken a lot of world records. Their presence (or lack thereof) would heavily influence the meaningfulness of a mean world record breaking number.

So, who are the runners with the most world records?

At the top is the Ethiopian Haile Gebreselasie, one of the most famous long-distance runners. In fact, the men with the most world records are all long-distance runners.

None of them are active athletes.

For the women, the exeption is right at the top: Marita Koch has not only broken the most running world records withing both genders, but is also the only short-distance runner.

For men and women there were several athletes who have broken a world record many times, which influences the total "world-record-breakiness" of their respective disciplines. Since none of them are active anymore, it lowers the probability of seeing a new male long-distance records or seeing a new female short- or middle-distance record to a certain extent. But since the majority of the world records were not broken by a few, single people, it still makes sence to calculate how many days it takes on average within a disciple for a world record to be broken.

This last one shows that you should be especially excited for the women's 10.000 meter run! But other long-distance runs are also very likely to be record-worthy!

Surprisingly, this graph also predicts a high probability for a new record within the women's short distances. This could be explained by the fact that even though there were not too many individuals with many world records, many athletes from the former Soviet Union or East Germany were successful in these categories. This could be interpreted as a "semi-spike". Nowadays, either the training style withing the newly formed countries has changed or the relevance of the sport has sunk.

Sports (luckily) can't be predicted, though. But if you like to bet (with your friends and not a company, preferrably), this overview might give you an inkling who's name to shout when the gun shot goes off.

This being said: Happy Olympics and good luck to all the athletes!
